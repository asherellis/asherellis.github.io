Air Force intelligence officer. M.S.E. student in Computer and Information Science at the University of Pennsylvania through the AF Strategic Scholars Program — one of ten officers selected nationally. Graduate Student Researcher at MIT Lincoln Laboratory, where I led a student team building an automated computer vision training platform for Air Force Special Operations Command through the SOCOM Ignite program.

I studied Applied Mathematics at Yale, graduating in 2025 with a thesis on adversarially robust computer vision models for synthetic aperture radar imagery. As a graduate student, I have been a research assistant at the Forecasting Research Institute, building AI-assisted geopolitical forecasting and surveillance pipelines. Before that, I researched AI capabilities at Stanford HAI and interned at In-Q-Tel, the Special Competitive Studies Project, Rhombus Power, and Beta Technologies — where I flew planes and helicopters.

My writing has appeared in the Wall Street Journal, East Asia Forum, the Yale Daily News, and the Special Competitive Studies Project. I ran Archipelago Notes, a newsletter on Indonesian geopolitics, for several years. My paper "Prediction Markets in National Security" was a finalist at the MIT-Harvard Technology & National Security Conference.

I've traveled to over 45 countries — including Ukraine during the war, China, Taiwan, and Indonesia, where I attended the inauguration of Nusantara, the country's new capital. I spent a summer on a backcountry trail crew in the Cascade Range and have ridden Amtrak across the country. I do Jewish genealogy as a hobby, tracing family roots across Lithuania, Belarus, and Morocco. I enjoy hiking, photography, and road trips. You can reach me by email or X.
