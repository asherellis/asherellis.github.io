My family is Jewish on both sides — Ashkenazi and Sephardic. The research here covers six generations, tracing lines from the shtetls of Belarus and Lithuania to the mellah of Casablanca, from Brooklyn tenements to Los Angeles.

Seven of eight great-grandparents are fully documented. The eighth — my paternal great-grandfather Nathan Edward Ellis — remains the one open mystery, somewhere between England and Eastern Europe.
