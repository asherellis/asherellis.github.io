Forecasting & AI-Assisted Intelligence

  • LEAP Surveillance Pipeline
    Forecasting Research Institute · 2025–present

    Research assistant at FRI building an AI-assisted geopolitical forecasting pipeline. The system takes a corpus of forecasting questions, deploys large language models to surveil open-source information, and tracks resolution-relevant developments over time. Work spans prompt engineering, LLM benchmarking, and integrating prediction market signals alongside model and human forecaster output.

  • Prediction Markets in National Security
    MIT-Harvard Technology & National Security Conference (finalist) · April 2026

    Paper arguing that prediction markets offer genuine analytic value for national security but only if interpreted and governed carefully. Develops a three-tier threat framework — structural limitations, adversarial manipulation, and security externalities — and proposes analytic tradecraft and governance responses. PDF.

Computer Vision & Defense AI

  • MINDFLARe — Automated CV Training Platform for AFSOC
    MIT Lincoln Laboratory / SOCOM Ignite · 2025–present

    Led a student team developing an automated computer vision training platform for Air Force Special Operations Command. The system selects permissively licensed pretrained backbones, attaches custom detection heads trained on DoD-compatible datasets, and distills lightweight models for edge deployment. Work addresses a real constraint in defense AI: most widely-used pretrained weights are encumbered by licenses incompatible with operational military use.

  • Adversarial Robustness in CNNs for Synthetic Aperture Radar Imagery
    Yale University senior thesis · 2025

    Senior thesis developing adversarially robust computer vision models for synthetic aperture radar (SAR) imagery. SAR is a sensing modality increasingly central to ISR missions; adversarial attacks on SAR classifiers represent an understudied failure mode with real operational implications.
